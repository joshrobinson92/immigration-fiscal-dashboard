Cumulative Taxes Paid vs. Government Spending (1994–2023)

Key Findings of the Study

  • Consistently Positive: In every single year from 1994 to 2023, the U.S. immigrant population generated more in federal, state, and local taxes than they cost in government services.
  • Deficit Reduction: Over this 30-year period, immigrants generated a net fiscal surplus of $10.6 Trillion. When accounting for interest savings on the national debt, the total fiscal savings reaches $14.5 Trillion.
  • Old-Age Benefit Savings: Immigrants cost 34% less per capita than U.S.-born citizens on old-age benefits (Social Security, Medicare, pensions) because they are younger and face legal barriers to eligibility.
  • Education Savings: Immigrants cost 53% less per capita on education because most arrive after completing school, providing the economy with trained workers without the training costs.
  • Debt Crisis Prevention: Without the positive fiscal contribution of immigrants and their children, U.S. public debt would have exceeded 205% of GDP by 2023 (nearly 75 percentage points higher).

Academic & Public Databases Used in the Study

The NASEM-Cato model distributes all federal, state, and local taxes and government spending to individual respondents. Below is the mapping of public databases used to construct the model:

Census Bureau CPS ASEC

Current Population Survey Annual Social and Economic Supplement (March 1994–2023)

Used to distribute the share of income, employment, school enrollment, family structure, and participation in welfare programs (Medicaid, SNAP, SSI, TANF, housing subsidies, EITC, CTC) between immigrants and natives.

Access Database

BEA NIPA Accounts

Bureau of Economic Analysis National Income and Product Accounts

The "source of truth" for total actual U.S. government current receipts (revenues) and current expenditures (spending). All micro-data totals are scaled proportionally to match these national aggregates, preventing over- or under-counting.

Access Database

Census Bureau ACS

American Community Survey (2006–2023)

Provides critical detailed metadata on institutionalized populations (such as those in prisons, jails, detention facilities, and nursing homes) and Limited English Proficiency (LEP) rates, which do not exist in detail in the CPS.

Access Database

AHRQ MEPS & CMS NHEA

Medical Expenditure Panel Survey & National Health Expenditure Accounts

The NHEA estimates per-enrollee medical spending by age and gender. The MEPS provides the only major health dataset tracking health expenditure shares by nativity, which is used to adjust Medicare and Medicaid spending distributions.

Access Database

Survey of School Finances

Census Bureau Annual Survey of School System Finances

Provides state-by-state actual per-pupil spending on public K-12 education. This is combined with individual student enrollment data and English proficiency weights to estimate specific K-12 schooling costs.

Access Database

OMB Historical Tables

White House Office of Management and Budget Historical Tables

Provides detailed aggregates for specific federal spending categories and tax credit outlays (such as Table 11.3 for outlays for payments to individuals and Table 3.2 for outlays by subfunction).

Access Database

Raw Study Dataset Viewer

This is the complete structured database extracted from the Cato Institute study and NASEM variables list. It contains population metadata, annual per-capita expenditures, and 30-year cumulative spending and tax items (in Billions) for US-born and Immigrant totals.
